Why Donate Food?

Every year, millions of people face food insecurity while tons of edible food goes to waste. Your donation can make a real difference in someone's life while reducing environmental impact.

How to Donate

Local Food Banks

Find food banks and shelters near you to donate excess food. Most accept non-perishable items and fresh produce.

Community Drives

Join local initiatives and food drives to distribute food to people in need in your community.

NGO Partners

Connect with organizations fighting hunger and food waste on a larger scale.

Donation Centers

Donation Guidelines

What to Donate

  • Non-perishable canned goods
  • Dry goods (rice, pasta, lentils)
  • Fresh fruits and vegetables
  • Packaged snacks
  • Baby food and formula
  • Sealed beverages

What NOT to Donate

  • Expired food items
  • Opened or unsealed packages
  • Home-cooked meals (without approval)
  • Damaged cans or containers
  • Alcohol
  • Items past their prime

Best Practices

  • Check expiration dates before donating
  • Package items securely
  • Sort items by category
  • Call ahead to confirm needs
  • Keep refrigerated items cold during transport
  • Donate regularly, not just during holidays
